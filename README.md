# COVID-19 Vaccination vs. Death Rates Statistical Analysis 📊🦠

A data-driven statistical analysis project investigating the correlation and impact of global COVID-19 vaccination dosages on recorded mortality rates. This project utilizes descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis to evaluate the efficacy of vaccination campaigns across various global datasets.

## 🚀 Research Objectives
* **Correlation Analysis:** Determine the strength and direction of the statistical relationship between vaccination percentages and death rates.
* **Hypothesis Testing:** Conduct rigorous statistical testing to verify if declines in mortality rates post-vaccination deployment are statistically significant.
* **Predictive Modeling:** Evaluate linear regression trends to understand mortality rate predictability based on immunization benchmarks.

---

## 🛠️ Data Science Tools & Methodologies
* **Analysis Environment:** SPSS / Excel / Python (Pandas & SciPy)
* **Statistical Methods:** Pearson/Spearman Correlation Coefficient, Linear Regression Modeling, Independent T-Tests, and Descriptive Statistics.
* **Visualizations:** Scatter plots with regression lines, trend charts, and distribution histograms.

---

## 📋 Core Findings & Analysis

### 🔹 Statistical Highlights
1. **Descriptive Summary:** Aggregation of global country-by-country data tracking total cases, total deaths, and full vaccination coverage tracking metrics.
2. **Correlation Matrix:** Evaluation mapping out how higher vaccination saturation structurally interfaces with lower overall case-fatality ratios.
3. **Regression Insights:** Linear slope estimations tracking the real-world reduction behavior of extreme clinical outcomes following widespread immunization.

### 🔹 Project Constraints & Data Integrity
* **Data Standardization:** Accounting for missing or under-reported country diagnostics to avoid demographic bias.
* **Temporal Tracking:** Structuring comparisons across identical waves of infection cycles to ensure valid computational conclusions.
